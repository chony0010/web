Rapid Decline in Wildlife Populations

Threats of Extinction

Spotlight on Endangered Animals

Biodiversity is essential for maintaining Earth's health, yet it currently faces unprecedented threats. Over 42,100 species are endangered, representing about 28% of all evaluated species. This alarming statistic underscores the urgent need for action against the main causes of extinction: habitat destruction, climate change, and resource overexploitation. The survival of diverse species is crucial not only for ecosystem balance but also for human well-being. Healthy ecosystems provide essential services such as clean air, water, and food.

Habitat Destruction Habitat loss is a primary cause of extinction, driven by urbanization, agriculture, and deforestation. Forests, which cover about 31% of global land, are decreasing by around 10 million hectares yearly, significantly endangering biodiversity.
Climate Change Climate change is projected to put up to one million species at risk of extinction by 2050. With a temperature rise of 1.5°C to 3°C, up to 28% of terrestrial species could face extinction.
Overexploitation Unsustainable fishing practices have resulted in 33% of fish species being overexploited, increasing stress on marine ecosystems.
Pollution Pollution is a major driver of biodiversity loss, with water, air, and soil contamination severely impacting ecosystems. The release of chemicals and heavy metals threatens the survival of freshwater species and plants, raising their risk of extinction.
